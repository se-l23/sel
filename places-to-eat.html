Japan offers an incredible array of dining experiences, from high-end restaurants to casual street food stalls and cozy cafes. For restaurants, Japan is famous for dishes like sushi, ramen, and tempura. Sushi lovers can indulge at upscale spots like Sukiyabashi Jiro in Tokyo or visit casual conveyor belt sushi places like Sushiro. Ramen shops, such as Ippudo in Tokyo, serve rich broths and perfectly cooked noodles, while kaiseki restaurants, like Gion Karyo in Kyoto, offer exquisite multi-course meals that showcase traditional Japanese cuisine at its finest. Tempura enthusiasts will enjoy dining at places like Tenshige, where fresh, crispy tempura is the highlight.

Street food in Japan is vibrant and easily accessible, especially in areas like Osaka and Tokyo. Popular treats include takoyaki (octopus-filled balls) and okonomiyaki, a savory pancake packed with vegetables, seafood, or meat. You can find these street foods in busy districts like Dotonbori in Osaka. Other must-try items include yakisoba (fried noodles), taiyaki (fish-shaped pastries filled with sweet fillings), and crispy karaage (fried chicken), which are widely available in food stalls and markets.
